📐 Probability Calculation Process

1 Setting Base Probability
Starting with the base probability based on historical war patterns. The 2-4 month period has the highest initial probability (25%) based on the average duration of modern wars.
Base: within 1M(12%), 1-2M(22%), 2-4M(25%), 4-6M(18%), 6-9M(12%), 9-12M(7%), 12M+(4%)
2 Applying Military Situation Factors
Adjusting based on actual war progress - air superiority, missile capability destruction rate, ground operations status, etc. If Israel has destroyed 80% of air defenses, the short-term end probability increases.
Example: Air superiority secured → within 1 month +4%p, 1-2 months +5%p
3 Diplomatic/Political Signal Analysis
Reflecting statements from government leaders, UN resolutions, backchannel negotiation intelligence, etc. Trump's "4-5 weeks" prediction statement is an important signal in itself.
Example: Iran CIA negotiation attempt → within 1 month +5%p, 1-2 months +6%p
4 Real-time News Signal Measurement
Automatically collecting news articles from 4 media RSS feeds and counting keyword occurrences. If ceasefire-related articles are more than escalation-related ones, it's a positive signal for early war end.
Example: Ceasefire mentions > Escalation mentions → within 1 month +5%p
5 Economic Pressure Variables
Assessing global economic impact including the Strait of Hormuz blockade. As oil prices soar and international pressure for resolution increases, the short-term end probability rises.
6 Normalization
After applying all factors, normalizing so the total probability sums to exactly 100%. Each period's probability is adjusted proportionally from the original ratio.
Formula: Final probability = (Adjusted value / Total sum) × 100
